# Draft Strategy Memo: Accelerating Infosys Towards an AI-Native Enterprise

*Author: [Redacted], Member of the Executive Leadership Team, Infosys Limited (INFY)*
*Audience: Infosys Board of Directors and Executive Council*
*Date: June 2026*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Infosys Limited ("Infosys") is at an inflection point where artificial intelligence (AI), particularly generative and agentic AI, is reshaping global IT services and business operations. Over the past two years, we have launched Infosys Topaz™, unveiled an AI First Value Framework, and introduced more than 200 enterprise AI agents in partnership with hyperscalers such as Google Cloud, positioning Infosys as a leading AI-first services provider. Our FY25 Integrated Annual Report already frames our narrative as "AI Your Enterprise," signaling that AI is central to our strategy, not a side offering.

However, we are currently **AI-forward in the market but not yet AI-native in our own operating model**. While we help clients design and execute AI programs, many of our internal processes, delivery models, and decision-making mechanisms still reflect a pre-AI era. To maintain our competitive edge against global peers and emerging AI-native consultancies, Infosys must fully commit to becoming an **AI-native enterprise** across three dimensions:

1. **AI-native client value**: Recasting core offerings and deal constructs around AI-first outcomes, not just hours and FTEs.
2. **AI-native operations**: Embedding agentic AI into our delivery, governance, and support functions to structurally improve margins and agility.
3. **AI-native culture and governance**: Ensuring responsible, trusted, and pervasive AI usage across the organization, aligned with our values and regulatory obligations.

The intent of this memo is to:

- Make a clear strategic case for Infosys to push decisively towards being an AI-native enterprise.
- Propose a **three-year roadmap** anchored on Infosys Topaz™, our AI First Value Framework, and AI Strategy + Value Office.
- Define explicit commitments, KPIs, and governance mechanisms for execution.

## 2. Context: Where Infosys Stands on AI Today

### 2.1 External Market Context

Industry estimates indicate that AI-first services represent an incremental addressable opportunity of **USD 300–400 billion by 2030**. Within this, enterprise demand is shifting from experimentation (POCs and pilots) to productionized AI platforms, agentic workflows, and AI-enabled business model transformation.

Key market trends relevant to Infosys:

- **Agentic AI and smaller, specialized models**: Enterprises are moving away from monolithic models to smaller, domain-specific, and agentic systems orchestrating multiple AI tools and services.
- **AI-first modernization**: Organizations are using AI to reverse engineer, document, and incrementally modernize legacy estates, replacing traditional application management with AI-assisted lifecycle management.
- **AI trust, governance, and regulation**: AI regulations and sectoral compliance expectations are accelerating, making responsible AI practices a differentiator in AI services.

### 2.2 Infosys’s Current AI Assets and Strategy

Infosys has made significant strategic investments in AI:

- **Infosys Topaz™**: An AI-first set of services, platforms, and solutions with **12,000+ AI assets, 150+ pre-trained AI models, and 10+ AI platforms**, built on our applied AI framework and Infosys Cobalt cloud ecosystem.
- **AI First Value Framework**: A two-pronged AI strategy to both (a) capture new AI-first services demand across defined value pools (AI Strategy & Engineering, Data for AI, Process AI, Agentic Legacy Modernization, Physical AI, AI Trust) and (b) augment existing services with AI for wallet share expansion.
- **AI Strategy + Value Office**: A structured offering that helps clients align AI initiatives with business value realization, governance, and risk management.
- **Enterprise AI Agents and Topaz Fabric**: Launch of over **200 enterprise AI agents** and the **Topaz Fabric™** stack—composable AI agents, services, models, and flows designed to unify IT service delivery across enterprise landscapes.

Financially, in FY25 Infosys delivered **4.2% revenue growth in constant currency with operating margin of 21.1% and record free cash flow of ₹34,549 crore**, despite a cautious demand environment. The market expects AI-led projects to contribute to margin uplift of 50–100 basis points as they scale into production over the next 12–24 months.

**Conclusion:** We have the right platforms, partnerships, and financial capacity. Yet, we are still early in re-architecting Infosys itself as an AI-native enterprise.

## 3. Why Infosys Must Become AI-Native (Not Just AI-Enabled)

### 3.1 Strategic Rationale

1. **Client expectations have shifted to AI-first transformation partners**
   Clients increasingly seek partners who can operate AI-native themselves—embodying the practices they prescribe. Platforms like Topaz™ and our AI First Value Framework are compelling, but clients will compare our internal AI maturity with that of cloud hyperscalers and born-AI consultancies.

2. **Margin and productivity pressure**
   Traditional labor-arbitrage models face structural headwinds. AI-native delivery can materially compress cycle times, reduce manual effort in testing, documentation, and operations, and unlock sustainable margin improvements beyond incremental cost optimization.

3. **Talent and brand positioning**
   The next generation of AI talent will prefer organizations where AI is part of everyday work, not a separate “innovation lab.” Being AI-native strengthens our employer brand and retention.

### 3.2 Risk of Inaction

- **Commoditization of legacy services**: If we keep AI at the periphery, we risk being perceived as a traditional SI with AI wrappers rather than a true AI-native partner.
- **Loss of strategic deals**: Large transformation deals increasingly include AI-native governance, agentic automation, and AI-first operations as table stakes.
- **Internal complexity**: Without AI-native operations, our internal complexity and cost base will continue to increase as we scale AI services externally.

## 4. Strategic Pillars for an AI-Native Infosys

We propose three strategic pillars with aligned initiatives and KPIs.

### 4.1 Pillar 1: AI-Native Client Value

**Objective:** Re-architect Infosys’s client offerings so that AI is embedded in the core of deal design, delivery, and commercial constructs.

Key initiatives:

1. **AI-First Deal Framework**
   - Make the AI First Value Framework the default lens for all strategic RFPs and transformation proposals, ensuring that at least one of the AI value pools is explicitly addressed in every large deal.
   - Require an AI value storyboard (use-case backlog + value estimation) as part of every proposal above a defined deal size threshold.

2. **Topaz-Embedded Service Lines**
   - Each major service line (Cloud, Digital, Applications, Infrastructure, BPM) must define a **Topaz-embedded variant** for its top 3 offerings (e.g., AI-driven app modernization, AI-augmented testing, AI-enabled operations).
   - Partner with the AI Strategy + Value Office to industrialize AI value measurement frameworks for these offerings.

3. **Agentic Solutions Catalogue**
   - Scale the 200+ Topaz AI agents into a **curated catalogue** of certified use cases by industry and function (e.g., claims processing, KYC, supply chain exceptions), each with reference architectures and estimated business impact.
   - Embed these into our proposal tooling so deal teams can rapidly assemble AI-native solution stacks.

**Suggested KPIs (3-year view):**

- ≥ 60% of large deals (> defined threshold) include an AI-first value storyboard.
- ≥ 40% of Infosys revenue influenced by Topaz-branded or AI-native offerings.
- Reference case library of ≥ 300 deployed AI agents in production across clients.

### 4.2 Pillar 2: AI-Native Operations (Run Infosys on Infosys Topaz™)

**Objective:** Transform Infosys’s own operations, delivery, and governance using Topaz and our AI agents, as a living showcase of AI-native practices.

Key initiatives:

1. **Agentic Delivery Operations**
   - Deploy internal Topaz agents to support core delivery workflows: requirement analysis, code review, regression testing, defect triage, documentation generation, and release governance.
   - Start with a set of flagship accounts and Infosys internal platforms to create high-visibility exemplars.

2. **AI-Augmented Governance & Risk**
   - Use AI agents to continuously monitor delivery risks, contractual obligations, and SLA adherence across projects, generating early-warning signals for account leaders.
   - Integrate AI trust and responsible AI checks into our internal change management and quality assurance processes.

3. **AI-Enhanced Business Support Functions**
   - Apply AI to Finance, HR, and Procurement: forecasting, workforce planning, skills matching, policy compliance, vendor risk management, and employee experience journeys.
   - Partner with the AI Strategy + Value Office to quantify internal productivity gains, using the same value frameworks we apply to clients.

**Suggested KPIs:**

- ≥ 20% reduction in average project documentation effort through AI assistance.
- ≥ 15% reduction in incident resolution time for AI-enabled internal platforms.
- AI-based early warning applied to ≥ 50% of large accounts.

### 4.3 Pillar 3: AI-Native Culture, Talent, and Governance

**Objective:** Build a pervasive, responsible AI culture backed by strong governance, consistent with Infosys’s values and regulatory expectations.

Key initiatives:

1. **AI Fluency for All**
   - Launch an Infosys-wide AI fluency program, integrating Topaz learning paths, with role-specific tracks for developers, architects, project managers, and business consultants.
   - Target 100% of delivery and client-facing talent to complete baseline AI literacy within 12–18 months.

2. **AI Skills and Career Architecture**
   - Define AI-specific competency frameworks and career paths (AI engineer, AI architect, AI governance specialist, AI product manager).
   - Integrate AI competency levels into performance and promotion criteria for relevant roles.

3. **AI Trust & Ethics by Design**
   - Operationalize the AI Trust value pool from the AI First Value Framework into internal policies: data governance, bias testing, model monitoring, incident response.
   - Create an **AI Ethics & Risk Council** reporting to the Board, integrating cross-functional leaders (Legal, Risk, Delivery, HR, CISO).

**Suggested KPIs:**

- ≥ 80% of delivery workforce AI-fluent (as measured by internal certification).
- AI Trust controls embedded in 100% of AI initiatives.
- Zero material regulatory non-compliance incidents related to AI in the period.

## 5. Enablers: Data, Platforms, and Partnerships

To be AI-native, Infosys must solidify three critical enablers.

### 5.1 AI-Ready Data Foundation

Our own enterprise data must be AI-ready, in line with our Data for AI value pool:

- Unified, governed data layer integrating delivery, finance, HR, sales, and knowledge repositories.
- Data fingerprinting and synthetic data where required to ensure privacy and regulatory compliance.
- Clear data product ownership with accountable stewards.

### 5.2 Topaz as the Unified AI Operating Layer

Topaz must function as the **central AI operating system** for Infosys:

- All internal AI solutions should preferentially adopt Topaz patterns, components, and governance.
- Establish a Topaz Center of Excellence responsible for reusable assets, internal reference architectures, and multi-cloud support.

### 5.3 Strategic Ecosystem Partnerships

We must continue to deepen partnerships with leading AI infrastructure and model providers:

- Expand work with hyperscalers (Google Cloud, Azure, AWS) and specialized AI model providers as appropriate.
- Align joint go-to-market programs with our AI-native narrative.

## 6. Execution Roadmap (2026–2029)

### Phase 1 (0–12 months): Align, Pilot, and Signal

- Declare AI-native transformation as a board-level strategic priority for Infosys.
- Launch **AI Native Infosys Program** under a dedicated executive sponsor.
- Select 3–5 flagship internal programs (e.g., internal delivery platform, finance forecasting, HR talent marketplace) to run with Topaz AI agents end-to-end.
- Integrate AI First Value Framework into all strategic proposal templates and deal coaching.
- Stand up the AI Ethics & Risk Council.

### Phase 2 (12–24 months): Scale and Standardize

- Roll out agentic AI support across a larger portfolio of delivery and support functions.
- Codify AI-native operating patterns and publish internal playbooks.
- Embed AI Trust, data governance, and AI risk checks into all internal and client-facing AI initiatives.
- Begin formal reporting on AI-native progress and AI-driven value in the Integrated Annual Report.

### Phase 3 (24–36 months): Institutionalize

- Make AI-native practices the default mode of operation for Infosys.
- Embed AI-native metrics into executive scorecards and incentive structures.
- Position Infosys externally as an AI-native enterprise through thought leadership, client references, and ecosystem narratives.

## 7. Risks and Mitigations

1. **Execution Complexity**
   - *Risk*: Large-scale AI adoption may create duplication and technical debt if unmanaged.
   - *Mitigation*: Use Topaz as the unifying AI layer; mandate architecture reviews via the Topaz CoE.

2. **Change Fatigue**
   - *Risk*: Workforce overload due to concurrent transformations (cloud, agile, AI).
   - *Mitigation*: Phased rollout, focused pilots, clear communication of benefits, and role-based training pathways.

3. **Regulatory and Ethical Concerns**
   - *Risk*: AI-related incidents could harm Infosys’s reputation and client trust.
   - *Mitigation*: Strong AI Trust framework, AI Ethics & Risk Council oversight, proactive risk management embedded into AI lifecycle.

4. **Market Perception Risk**
   - *Risk*: If we over-promise on AI-native capabilities without internal proof, we risk credibility.
   - *Mitigation*: Sequence marketing with internal adoption; prioritize "run Infosys on Infosys" case studies before aggressive external claims.

---

## 8. Recommendations and Ask to the Board

I recommend the Board of Infosys Limited endorse the following:

1. **Formal declaration** of AI-native transformation as a strategic pillar in Infosys’s corporate strategy and Integrated Annual Report.
2. **Creation of an AI Native Infosys Program**, sponsored by a member of the Executive Council, with cross-functional leadership from Delivery, HR, Finance, Risk, and the Topaz organization.
3. **Allocation of a ring-fenced multi-year investment pool** dedicated to AI-native initiatives, including internal modernization, AI talent development, and Topaz platform enhancements.
4. **Mandate that all large strategic deals include an AI-first value assessment** using our AI First Value Framework and, where appropriate, Topaz agents.
5. **Establishment of an AI Ethics & Risk Council** to oversee AI governance across Infosys’s internal and client-facing AI initiatives.

By committing decisively to becoming an AI-native enterprise, Infosys can:

- Strengthen its position as a global leader in AI-first digital services.
- Enhance margins and productivity through structural AI-led efficiencies.
- Attract and retain top AI talent, reinforcing its brand as an innovation-led, values-driven organization.

**Infosys is uniquely positioned to lead the AI-native enterprise era.** This is the moment to align our internal reality with our external promise.
